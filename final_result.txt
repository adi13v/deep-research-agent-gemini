UK bases in Bahrain, Qatar, and Cyprus have also been attacked, leading to the defensive deployment of the RAF. **Diplomatic Standoff and Nuclear Concerns**
Diplomatic negotiations between the U.S. and Iran regarding Iran's nuclear program failed in early February 2026. President Trump has since demanded Iran's "unconditional surrender" and the selection of a new leader, stating that it is "too late" for negotiations. Iran's foreign minister, Abbas Araghchi, publicly rejected these demands, asserting Tehran is not seeking a ceasefire or negotiations. The U.S. and Israel maintain their military actions aim to prevent Iran from developing nuclear weapons, citing Iran's continued expansion of its nuclear and ballistic missile programs [apnews](https://vertexaisearch.cloud.google.com/grounding-api-redirect/AUZIYQFSJPWvX0OhxF-gSB9JsZgyn5XP7Pbus2utdkce_D4vCBXHbm1XQDYZNsPR_eDY9Qa_alBuK-yG70CERtSIHyVOrW-FCTyHup6yhA2lPnLCgBltGrXrjQYipjCgEB8l0HwB9wwiDEbjMFrKDShmLsXDzjmKpu7ncVEO4jHuPNwkRiONESwN0sYMfI77AvZezfbhZoJ3yTxg4P9KRpsfagG8kYgHZ0P-cXE-).

Prior to recent airstrikes, the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) estimated Iran possessed 441 kilograms of 60% enriched uranium, sufficient for 10 nuclear weapons.
